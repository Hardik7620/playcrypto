Keeping Minors Out of the Game

Not every door should be open to everyone. Some thresholds are meant to be guarded.

At PlayCrypto365, access begins with age. Every user must be at least 18 years old — no exceptions. We don’t just ask for a birthdate. We verify it. Our technology screens every account for signs of underage use, and if we find a red flag, we act immediately.

There’s no second chance here. If an account is discovered to belong to a minor, we don’t negotiate. The account is closed. Winnings are forfeited. That’s our line in the sand.

We also never advertise to minors. Not online, not offline, not subtly or overtly. If you're underage, this is not your world — and we’re here to make sure it stays that way.


Your Role: Guard the Gate

Even the most secure systems need human allies. If there are children in your home, your vigilance matters.

Here’s how you can help protect your PlayCrypto365 account:

  • Keep your password and login details private

  • Log out every time you step away from your device

  • Never store credit card information on shared devices

  • Educate minors about the risks of gambling

Responsible Gambling: The Discipline Behind the Game

Gambling, at its best, is a form of entertainment. 

We encourage you to ask yourself the below questions to help you stay on the safe side of the line:

  • Are you spending only what you can afford to lose?

  • Are you chasing losses to "even the score"?

  • Are you treating gambling as fun, or as a way out of financial pressure?

  • Do you keep track of your time and money while playing?

  • Are you gambling sober and clear-minded?

The healthiest gamblers are the most self-aware. The difference between enjoyment and dependency is subtle — but it’s there. You’ll feel it in the moments when you hesitate, or when the game feels less like a choice and more like a compulsion.


When the Game Becomes the Problem

To help, Gamblers Anonymous created a set of questions. These aren’t designed to shame. They are designed to reflect. Read them carefully:

  • Have you skipped work or school because of gambling?

  • Has gambling created problems at home?

  • Do you feel guilt or regret after gambling?

  • Have you borrowed or sold items to gamble?

  • Do you gamble longer than planned?

  • Do you gamble to escape stress or sadness?

  • Have you felt an urge to gamble right after winning or losing?

  • Do you ever think about suicide because of gambling?

If you answered yes to seven or more, you’re not alone — but it might be time to talk to someone.


Stepping Away: Self-Exclusion and Breaks

If you feel that gambling is beginning to take control, reach out to us. We will help you disable your account — either temporarily or permanently. This is not a punishment. It’s protection.

And if you believe the problem is more than temporary, tell us. We will ensure your account remains closed, no matter what. Your well-being is more important than your balance.
